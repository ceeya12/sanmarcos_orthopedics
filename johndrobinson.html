Practice Overview

John D. Robinson, MD is a fellowship trained Sports Medicine Orthopedic Surgeon specializing in advanced knee and shoulder arthroscopy as well as shoulder, robotic knee and anterior hip joint replacement.

Completing his fellowship training with Tahoe Orthopedics and Mammoth Orthopedic Institute, he regularly treated complex mountain extremity injuries with an emphasis on the most innovative and minimally invasive techniques. His experience with cutting-edge procedures, such as ACL Repair, Bridge Enhanced ACL Restoration (BEAR), and shoulder arthroplasty using patient-specific computerized planning, provides a unique solution that preserves or restores native anatomy and function.

He is currently involved with the US Men’s and Women’s Ski and Snowboard Teams and will be assisting Texas State University as a co-team Physician. By providing care to patients from all walks of life – from Olympic medalists to recreational athletes, he understands the importance of developing a treatment plan that is personalized for each patient’s unique goals.

Dr. Robinson is married to his wife, Macie, who is a Pediatric Nurse Practitioner in Lockhart. They live in San Marcos and are passionate about living in and serving the surrounding communities. He is an avid outdoorsman that enjoys fly-fishing, archery, elk/deer hunting, skiing/snowboarding, football, basketball, pickleball, and golf.
